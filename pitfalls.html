Script pitfalls

The generic proof sheets exercise Latin well and everything else thinly. This page is the opposite: one test per known failure mode of the other scripts, each with a note saying what a failure looks like. Several are regressions this project has actually shipped — those name the incident. Items marked known are open issues; they should look wrong here until fixed, and this page is where to watch them get fixed.

Arabic — joining

Four forms of every joining letter. Each form should be a letter, not a plateau: a medial tooth is a small thing on a short connector, near one dot wide. Watch for cell-wide medials (the monospace inheritance), hairline seams where connectors meet, and letters that fail to join at all. Dal, reh, waw and alef rows join on one side only — if their empty cells show a connected form, the joining logic is wrong.

Known: ghain still reads as double-joined, so it does not sweep — watch the غ row.

Seen and beh carried on their flat runs. The final and isolated forms of these letters legitimately include a long flat stroke — a beh short form is 4–5 dots wide, a seen with its kashida 7–11. The teeth riding on that run must keep their drawn width: the fitter once scaled a seen final's teeth from ~290 units to 24, and this line is the regression test for it.

س ســلام ب باب ص صبر ش شمس تُرِكَتْ

Tatweel elongation. A deliberate kashida must be indistinguishable from the letters' own connectors: same height, same thickness, no step where they meet.

Arabic — dots and marks

Dot minimal pairs, joined. Rows differ only by their dots: every dot must be present, round, and sitting over (or under) its own letter through every joining form. A squashed or missing dot means a transform scaled a shape — that is how bá lost its dot to the connector compression.

ججج ححح خخخ
ددد ذذذ   ررر ززز
سسس ششش   صصص ضضض
ططط ظظظ   ععع غغغ   ففف ققق

Harakat, alone and stacked. Fatha, damma, kasra, sukun and tanwin sit clear of the letter; shadda stacks with a following fatha/damma above it, and a kasra conventionally moves below the shadda. Nothing may render beside its base — that was the combining-mark regression. Known: lám with doubled harakat stacks wrongly.

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ — مُحَمَّدٌ — رَبِّ — شَدَّةٌ — سُكُونْ

Mandatory ligatures. Lam-alef must form its ligature in all hamza variants, isolated and joined; the Allah ligature should read as one written word, not a fence of connectors.

لا لأ لإ لآ — بلا ملأ — الله والله لله ﷲ

Arabic — tails, lines, direction

Deep tails at normal line spacing. Watch the bottoms of ى ي س ع: they must neither clip nor collide with the next line. Known: final yeh descends to −1024 against a declared descender of −838, so yá is cut off in any renderer that clips to line metrics — this block is where that fix will show.

يحيى يسعى على مشى
يحيى يسعى على مشى
يحيى يسعى على مشى

Final sweeps tucking under. A final tail is allowed past its advance so it returns under the preceding letters — neighbouring words and punctuation must still not collide with it.

النفس عزيز، خبز جميل. قال: «انتهى».

Digits and punctuation. Arabic-Indic and Extended (Persian) digits align as figures; the comma, semicolon and question mark face the right way for RTL.

٠١٢٣٤٥٦٧٨٩ — ۰۱۲۳۴۵۶۷۸۹ — پ چ ژ گ ک ی — ؟ ؛ ، ٪

Mixed direction. Arabic and Latin on one line: spacing at every direction boundary stays even, and neither script visually outweighs the other.

قلم بديع — Qalam Badi — خط مشكین قلم Mishkín-Qalam والاسم الأعظم.

Transliteration Latin

Underdots and accented vowels. The Bahá'í transliteration alphabet in running text. Underdots on ḥ ṣ ṭ ẓ ḍ must be the same dot module as everything else, must not collide with descenders on the line below, and must survive an underline.

Bahá'u'lláh — Ḥuqúqu'lláh — Kitáb-i-Aqdas — Ṭihrán — maẓharu'ẓ-ẓuhúr, aṣ-Ṣalát

Ayin, hamza and the apostrophes. ʿ (U+02BF) and ʾ (U+02BE) stand directly beside ' and ' in this orthography and must match them in size and position. Known: ʿ is oversized (604×680 against the quote's 366×555) — position is fixed, size is not.

ʿAbduʾl-Bahá — 'Abdu'l-Bahá — maʿa — ma'a — ʾabhá — 'abhá

Cyrillic

Descenders and diacritics. The tails of д ц щ and both cases of Й/Ё must clear the line below; the breve and diaeresis must sit centred, not beside (the same class of bug as the Arabic combining marks).

дом царь щука руж — Дц Щц Уу Фф — Ёлка ёж Йод майка

Confusables hold their shape. б is not 6, З is not 3, Ч is not 4; and the letters shared with Latin (а е о р с х) should sit on the same rhythm as their Latin twins, since both scripts came through the same proportional fit.

б6 З3 Ч4 — асеорх / aceopx — НАВСЕГДА навсегда

Beyond Russian. Serbian, Macedonian and Ukrainian letters at the same color as the core set. (Serbian's localized italic forms of б г д п т are not implemented — if a browser claims them here in italic, it is synthesizing.)

Ђђ Љљ Њњ Ћћ Џџ — Ґґ Єє Іі Її

Greek

Final sigma and tonos. ς only at word end; every accented vowel keeps its tonos clear of the letter; dialytika and tonos combine on ΐ ΰ without colliding.

λόγος σοφός — ά έ ή ί ό ύ ώ — ϊ ϋ ΐ ΰ — προΐσταμαι

Capitals and punctuation. Accented capitals carry the tonos at the upper left, not on top; the Greek question mark is the semicolon shape, and the ano teleia sits at x-height.

Άγιος Έρως Ήλιος Ίδη Όρος Ύδρα Ώρα — τί λέγεις; — ἓν οἶδα· οὐδὲν οἶδα

Polytonic. The font covers polytonic Greek; breathings and accents must combine cleanly or the coverage is a false promise.

Ἐν ἀρχῇ ἦν ὁ λόγος, καὶ ὁ λόγος ἦν πρὸς τὸν θεόν.

Cross-script rhythm

One line, four scripts. Set at one size, no script should read darker, larger or looser than the others — the shared dot module is the claim; this line is the check.
